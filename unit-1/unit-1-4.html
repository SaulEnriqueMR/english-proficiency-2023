1.4 Grammar. Suffixies

A suffix is a handy little thing that goes at the end of a word to change it into a different part of speech, for example, verb to noun, noun to adjective, adjective to adverb, or similar.

Read the next link if you want further explanation and examples: Suffixes.

Stop and think

  • What suffixes are used in Spanish, how do they change words?
  • How many noun suffixes do you know in English?
  • Are there any parallel constructions?

Recording collocations

There are so many collocations that a list of them would take up pages and pages. Instead, you can use an online dictionary to check your answers: Online Collocation Dictionary.

Now you can start to keep a list of interesting collocations in your notebook. When you find a new one, check it in the dictionary and add alternatives.

Example:

secret – burning – lifelong – ambition.
